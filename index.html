A podcast for comedy music directors

About

Comedy Keys is a podcast for people in the comedy arts with an interest in music direction. Whether you are an experienced music director looking for some new ideas, a musician who wants to get involved in sketch and improv, or an improviser who decided to learn an instrument to accompany your shows, this podcast is for you!

New episodes are released monthly on the first Sunday of the month. We'll have interviews, new techniques, helpful tips, and whatever else you might want to learn - just tell us what you want to hear!

Episodes

Epsiode 1: Introduction and four chords

Talking about the start of this podcast and how to start music directing with a simple but popular four chord song. Scott explains the motivation behind the podcast, what you can expect to hear from future episodes, and how he got in to music direction in the first place - with the four chord song. If you can learn just four chords on any instrument, you can improvise a song!

Epsiode 2: Vocal warmups

with Gwen Coburn

In our first guest interview, we talk with Gwen who has a degree in music pedagogy and is a cast member of ImprovBoston's Family Show, an entirely improvised musical. Gwen discusses how we as performers can give ourselves a leg up on vocal warmups and vocal health, and how a music director can set their cast up for vocal success in a show.

Epsiode 3: Underscoring

with Steve Sarro

Steve is a music director at ImprovBoston and at Art Barn youth theater. In this episode, Steve gives us his favorite tricks of the trade when it comes to improvised underscoring. Deciding both what to play and when to play can be a daunting task but rewarding when done well!
